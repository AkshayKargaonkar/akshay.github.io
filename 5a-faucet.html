5a-faucet: A water saving innovation with enhanced mobility

During my time at DG TAKANO, I had the privilege of contributing to the 5a-Faucet project, an exciting collaboration with the renowned industrial design firm Whipsaw. This project aimed to create an innovative, water-saving faucet with a user-focused design.

This faucet, named "5a" for the five degrees of freedom it offers, provides unparalleled spatial coverage, allowing the faucet-head, acting as the end-effector, to reach a broader operational area. This enhances usability and functionality, making it ideal for various applications.

My primary role in the project focused on the design and development of the faucet-head, where I engineered a sophisticated switching mechanism to seamlessly alternate between flow modes. In addition, I devised a novel aerated flow design that optimizes water efficiency without compromising user satisfaction.

To ensure reliability, I conducted extensive durability tests, simulating over 100,000 cycles of the different faucet's movement. These tests were crucial in assessing fatigue, wear, and long-term mechanical performance, ensuring the product's robustness and longevity.

More details about the product can be found on WHIPSAW's and DG Takano's websites.
